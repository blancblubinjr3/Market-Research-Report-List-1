<p><p>Redundant Arrays of Independent Drives (RAID) can be categorized into two primary types: software-based and hardware-based. Software-based RAID utilizes the server's CPU and system resources to manage disk arrays, offering flexibility and cost-effectiveness. In contrast, hardware-based RAID employs dedicated controllers to manage storage operations, providing enhanced performance and reliability, often with built-in caching. Both options serve to increase data redundancy and improve performance, but the choice depends on specific system requirements and available resources.</p></p>

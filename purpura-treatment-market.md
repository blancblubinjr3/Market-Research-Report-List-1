<p><p>The purpura treatment market is categorized into non-thrombocytopenic and thrombocytopenic types. Non-thrombocytopenic purpura is caused by vascular abnormalities, and treatments focus on managing symptoms and underlying conditions. In contrast, thrombocytopenic purpura results from low platelet counts, often requiring therapies such as corticosteroids, immunoglobulins, or platelet transfusions. The market reflects these distinctions in treatment options, highlighting the need for targeted approaches to address the specific causes and symptoms associated with each type of purpura.</p></p>
